About Me

Hi there! I’m Nitika, a communication design student from Mumbai.

My intent? Translate ideas into visuals that feel clear, intentional, and meaningful.

I’m currently studying at ISDI, ATLAS SkillTech University, exploring how form, type, and visuals influence perception and communication.

When I’m not designing, I’m usually capturing quick photos of everyday scenes or noticing the small details most people walk past.

I’m open to conversations, collaborations, and shared exploration.

For me, good design isn’t just seen, it’s understood.

Poster Designs

ECHOES OF THE CITY

Project Brief

The objective of this project was to conceptualize and write an original short play, and then
translate its core themes into a cohesive visual identity through a series of three posters.
The challenge was to build a narrative world, develop characters with emotional depth,
and visually express the tone, setting, and mood of the story.

Poster 1

Three young dreamers live separate lives in the city, each holding onto music in quiet, unspoken ways.

Poster 2

Their paths cross by chance, and through shared melodies, they begin to find connection and understanding.

Poster 3

Together, they return to the stage, blending their voices into one powerful performance of hope and belonging.

Campaign Design

NOTHING

Nothing is a brand that stands apart through minimal expression and thoughtful design.
The campaign centers on the metaphorical slogan
“When you have NOTHING in hand, you have EVERYTHING,”
highlighting how absence can create space for vision, power, and limitless possibility.
The visual language reflects clarity and restraint,
allowing meaning and experience to speak louder than form.

Project Type: Group Project (5 Members)

Photography

REFLECTIONS OF THE CITY

"Reflections of the City" captures the charm and beauty of urban life
through unexpected reflections. Using mirrors, water puddles,
car surfaces, and windows, I explored how the city's architecture
and nature create striking mirrored images.
Each photograph offers a new perspective, turning everyday scenes
into artistic compositions and inviting viewers to see the cityscape
through a lens of reflection.
